# Understanding the 'Credit Score vs Churn' Boxplot (ELI10 Style)

This boxplot shows how **CreditScore** differs between customers who **stayed** and those who **left (churned)** the bank.

---

### 🧩 What the Chart Shows

- **`Exited = 0`** → Customers who **stayed**.
- **`Exited = 1`** → Customers who **left**.
- **Y-axis:** CreditScore — how creditworthy or reliable each customer is.

Each box shows how credit scores are spread within each group.

---

### 📊 What You Can See

1. **The two boxes look almost the same.**  
   - Both have about the same median (~650).  
   - Both stretch from roughly **400 to 850**, covering a wide range of credit scores.  
   - The tops and bottoms of the boxes line up closely, showing very similar distributions.

2. **No noticeable gap or difference.**  
   - The shapes and positions of the boxes overlap almost completely.  
   - Unlike Age or Balance, there’s no visible shift between groups.

---

### 🧠 What This Means

This tells us:
> **Credit score doesn’t seem to affect whether a customer leaves or stays.**

People with low, medium, or high credit scores all churn at roughly the same rate.  
So creditworthiness isn’t a major reason for customers leaving this bank.

---

### ✅ ELI10 Summary

- Both groups have **similar credit score patterns**.
- **No big difference = weak or no relationship.**
- Therefore, **churn does not strongly depend on credit score** in this dataset.
